Initial Research (Quincy)

Problem statement
Need statement
School does a terrible job of preparing students for life after high school. One of the main skills that school fails to teach is financial literacy. Many students have terrible money management and financial skills and they are forced to learn this the hard way once they live on their own.
Teenagers need a way to learn about financial literacy so they can be prepared for life after high school and avoid financial mistakes that can end badly.
Evidence
Evidence
Evidence

  • Only six states require students to take a standalone personal finance course
  • Merely five states require students to pass the test in the financial literacy course to graduate

Public education doesn’t teach financial literacy, leading to lifelong problems for former students

Evidence
Evidence

Initial Research (Sommers)

Problem statement
Need statement
Despite popular belief and the idealized life of professional athletes, most end up filing for bankruptcy within a couple years of their careers ending. They often use unsustainable spending habits to fuel their lifestyle and when their revenue stream is gone they can no longer pay for how they live.
Current and future athletes need to be educated in financial literacy or else they are likely to go broke after finishing a career that could have generated generational wealth for them.
Evidence
  • “Being a professional athlete can be extremely rewarding and lucrative. However, a stark 78% of them will go broke after just 3 years of retirement, …. A "big factor," he said, is the significant amount of income they make in a short amount of time.”
  • We can begin by looking at the nature of how athletes earn their money. Athletes typically hit their earnings peak within a few years of finishing school. Across the three major American sports (MLB, NBA, NFL), the average career length is about 4.6 years. Thus, the average athlete’s career is over at a young age, and the typical player is only at their peak earnings for an extremely short period. Most never earn at the same level again. In contrast, the typical American reaches their earnings peak usually decades after finishing school and has accumulated a lifetime of knowledge about how to handle finances by that point. Some athletes may never think about an alternative career path for after retirement. Without formulating a “retirement plan” or proper budget, young athletes blow through their money.
  • Pro athletes who get into financial trouble have a few things in common when it comes to how they lost their fortunes, including having a small earnings window, a lack of financial knowledge, and overspending.
  • Reasons for athletes going broke “Misplaced Trust, Bad Advice, Household Troubles, Great Expectations, Lifestyle Debt, Greed, Vet Envy, Poor Financial Knowledge, and Negligence”
  • “Athletes who prioritize material wealth—cars, houses, jewelry, etc. —over financial discipline, strategic investing, and cultivating additional revenue sources tend to have money troubles.”
  • On average, NFL players last 3.3 years in the league, while NHL, NBA, and MLB careers last 3.5, 4.8, and 5.6 years, respectively.
