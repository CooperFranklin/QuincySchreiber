Initial Research (Quincy)

Problem statement
Need statement
School does a terrible job of preparing students for life after high school. One of the main skills that school fails to teach is financial literacy. Many students have terrible money management and financial skills and they are forced to learn this the hard way once they live on their own.
Teenagers need a way to learn about financial literacy so they can be prepared for life after high school and avoid financial mistakes that can end badly.
Evidence
Evidence
Evidence
  • Learning financial literacy leads to a better quality of life
  • The majority of school districts across the country include financial literacy in their educational standards, but many of them do not implement these standards fully
  • Five states do not have financial literacy included in K-12 standards
  • Only six states require students to take a standalone personal finance course
  • Merely five states require students to pass the test in the financial literacy course to graduate

    • Source
  • With over 80% of Americans unable to translate a tax form and one in four American student loan borrowers in default, there is no doubt that financial literacy is at an all-time low
  • Financial jargon is complex and difficult for the average American to fully understand, explains Cindy Couyoumjian. This has the effect of intimidating many adults and preventing them from asking critical questions about their financial well-being. We are simply taught that we need money to survive and put a roof over our heads. It can be too easy at times for the average consumer to feel overwhelmed with financial information.
  • As a result of the school system’s lack of modernization, experts who teach financial literacy are few and far between. Many teachers unfortunately lack the knowledge themselves on financial literacy

    • Source
  • Critics of school-based financial education have argued that teaching kids about money is the parents’ responsibility.
  • What they want is an investment in their communities and a real chance to make it on their own. And we can do our part by supporting neighborhood schools, providing training to teachers and reliable curriculum resources, to help the community help itself, not once, but for many years to come.
  • As a defined subject area, personal finance is still relatively young and evolving; but research such as the meta-analysis recently conducted by the Global Financial Literacy Excellence Center at George Washington University and independent research already support the promising effect.

Initial Research (Matteo)

Problem statement
Need statement
Public education doesn’t teach financial literacy, leading to lifelong problems for former students
Financial education should be a priority throughout public education as it leads to poor financial decision-making in adults
Evidence
Evidence
    As an Oakland Unified School District student, I have never had any form of financial literacy-related education. I notice the lack of knowledge around all things money whether it be poor spending habits or complete disregard for things like the stock market or retirement funds.
Financial education could be a huge step in the direction of helping aid the growing poverty cycle in America along with allowing more Americans to be financially free and less susceptible to financial insecurity later in life.
